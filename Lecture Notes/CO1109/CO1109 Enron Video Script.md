# CO1109 Enron — Video Script

**5 minutes total** | Reword in your own voice — don't read robotically

---

## Intro (30s)

"Hi, my name is Lakira Jayamanne and this presentation is about the Enron scandal — one of the biggest cases of corporate fraud in history. In 2001, Enron collapsed, taking 20,000 jobs with it and filing for the largest bankruptcy the US had ever seen. I'm going to cover three key ways they pulled it off, and for each one, an IT solution that could have stopped it."

---

## Point 1 — Mark-to-Market Fraud (1 min)

"The first method was mark-to-market accounting abuse. Normally this is a legitimate accounting technique, but Enron weaponised it. Say they signed a 20-year energy contract worth $500 million — they'd book that entire amount as profit immediately, on day one, even if not a single dollar had come in yet. This made their finances look incredible on paper while in reality they were haemorrhaging cash. An IT solution here would be a real-time cash flow monitoring system — it automatically compares what's being reported as profit against what cash is actually arriving. If the numbers don't match up, it raises an alert. Executives can't manually fudge the figures."

---

## Point 2 — Shell Companies (1 min)

"The second method was hiding debt through shell companies. CFO Andrew Fastow created hundreds of fake external companies — with names like LJM and Raptor — and transferred Enron's bad debts into them. On paper, the debt was gone. But these companies were secretly backed by Enron's own stock and run by Fastow himself. The debt never actually left. An IT solution would be an automated entity verification system — any company Enron does business with is instantly cross-referenced against registration databases and ownership records. If a supposedly external company is actually connected back to Enron executives, the system flags it and the transaction can't proceed."

---

## Point 3 — Arthur Andersen (1 min)

"The third and probably most disturbing factor was the failure of Arthur Andersen, Enron's auditing firm. Andersen were being paid a million dollars a week by Enron. They knew something was wrong but kept signing off on the accounts anyway. When investigators came knocking, Andersen employees shredded tonnes of documents and deleted thousands of emails. The firm collapsed entirely as a result. An IT solution here is an immutable audit trail — every financial document is recorded in a write-once system stored on an independent server. Nobody can delete or alter it. Shredding paper becomes irrelevant because everything is already backed up externally and permanently."

---

## Conclusion (30s)

"Enron's fraud lasted as long as it did because there was nothing in place to automatically catch it. The three IT solutions I've outlined — cash flow monitoring, entity verification, and immutable audit trails — wouldn't have needed advanced technology. They would just have made it impossible to hide the truth. Thanks for watching."
