	## Structure — 3 Key Points + IT Solutions
	
	### Point 1: Mark-to-Market Accounting Fraud
	- Enron booked *expected future profits* as current revenue today
	- Signed a 10-year $100m contract? Book the full $100m now, even if nothing earned yet
	- Massively inflated reported profits — pure fiction
	- **IT Solution: Real-time cash flow monitoring system**
	  - Flags gap between reported profits and actual cash coming in
	  - If you're booking $100m but zero cash arrives, system raises alert
	  - Removes ability for executives to manually override profit reporting
	
	### Point 2: SPE Shell Companies to Hide Debt
	- CFO Andrew Fastow created hundreds of fake shell companies (LJM, Raptor etc.)
	- Enron "sold" bad assets/debt to these companies to get them off the books
	- Shell companies were secretly owned/guaranteed by Enron itself — debt never actually left
	- **IT Solution: Third-party entity verification system**
	  - Any company on the books is automatically cross-referenced against external databases
	  - Checks company registrations, ownership records, independence from Enron
	  - Flags if a supposed "external" company is secretly linked back to Enron executives
	
	### Point 3: Failure of Auditors (Arthur Andersen)
	- Arthur Andersen were Enron's auditors — paid $1 million/week in fees
	- Knew something was wrong, kept signing off anyway
	- When SEC investigated, Andersen shredded tonnes of documents
	- Firm collapsed entirely as a result
	- **IT Solution: Immutable audit trail**
	  - Financial records written once, cannot be altered or deleted
	  - Stored independently of the company (third-party / government system)
	  - Real-time backup — shredding documents becomes impossible
	  - Auditors and executives can't destroy evidence

	## Background — The Story
	
	- Enron: Texas energy trading company, late 1990s
	- CEO Jeffrey Skilling transformed it into energy *trading* (buying/selling contracts)
	- Fortune magazine named it "America's Most Innovative Company" 6 years running
	- All fake — fraud collapsed in 2001, filed for bankruptcy (largest in US history at the time)
	- 20,000 employees lost jobs and pensions
	- Led to **Sarbanes-Oxley Act (2002)** — stricter financial reporting laws
	
	**Key people:**
	- Jeffrey Skilling — CEO, 24 years prison
	- Andrew Fastow — CFO, created shell companies, 6 years prison
	- Sherron Watkins — whistleblower, internal memo warning of collapse
	- Bethany McLean — journalist who first publicly questioned Enron's profits
