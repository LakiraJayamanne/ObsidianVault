# Polynomial 

 **A polynomial function degree *n*** is a function f: R -> R  defined by an algebraic expression of the form: 
 
$$
\Large f(x) = a_n x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1} + \dots + a_1 x + a_0
$$

where $n \in N$

Example: 

$$\Large f(x) = 2x^3 + 3x^2 + 6x + 5$$

$$\Large n=3 a_3=2 a_2=3 a1=6 a_0=5$$

# Degree and coefficients 

The **degree** polynomial is the highest exponent in the expression.

Example 

$$
\Large 2x^3 + 3x^2 + 6x +5
$$

The power of the first *x* here (3) is the degree.

Every number before any x are coefficient.
- This includes the 5, as 5 is the same as $5x^0$


1. Constant polynomial (degree 0):

$$
f(x) = 2
$$

2. Linear polynomial (degree 1):

$$
f(x) = 2x+1
$$

3. Quadratic polynomial (degree 2):

$$
\Large f(x) = x^2-4x+3
$$

4. Cubic polynomial (degree 3):

$$
\Large f(x) = 5x^3+2x^2-x+7
$$
